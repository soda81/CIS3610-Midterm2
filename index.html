Tarantino Films and Their Differences

A Blog Discussing Film Nuances

Reservoir Dogs - Quentin Tarantino's first Feature length film

Tarantino's first feature length film, a film that would set the tone for many of his films to come. Here began his trademark way of telling a story in a nonlinear form, with his equally trademark knack for stylized violence. Released in 1992, the film depicts a group of robbers, all strangers to each other, who are tasked with carrying out a robbery. The plot is fairly simple, but it is in how each character interacts with each other and how their personalities and morals clash that give the film its dramatic effect. The tone of the film fairly dark and suspenseful, with most of its runtime having a sense of urgency or impending doom.

Pulp Fiction - Tarantino's magnum opus

Regarded by many as Tarantino's greatest work, and with good reason. The film has had a massive influence on pop culture, and is referenced in many modern works today. Similar to Reservoir Dogs, it is known for its stylized violence and non-linear storyline but has a much lighter more comedic tone. This film is in my opinion what everyone thinks of when they hear "Tarantino" film. The plot, characters, dialog and pacing are everything a Tarantino film should be. It is no surprise that it is considered his best work.

Kill Bill - A slightly different formula

Compared to the previous movies discussed, Kill Bill is not your typical Tarantino movie. The first major is difference is that it is not a crime/thriller film, but instead focuses heavily on martial arts. The second major difference is that the film has a linear storyline unlike the previous two mentioned. Although they do have these differences, Tarantino's highly stylized method of filming still shines through.
